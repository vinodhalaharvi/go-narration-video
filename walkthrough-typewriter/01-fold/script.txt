[[title:Recursive Fold in Go]]
[[mode:typewriter granularity:line]]
[[file:fold.go reveal lines:1-8]] We start with the data. A node has a name, a number, and children that are themselves nodes. Three lines, recursive type.
[[reveal lines:10-18]] Now the operation. Fold takes a node, an initial value, and a function. It applies the function, then recurses into children. One generic function replaces every walk-this-tree you ever wrote.
[[reveal lines:20-25]] Predicates are functions from node to bool. Not interfaces. Just functions.
[[reveal lines:27-35]] And Find composes them. Fold the tree, accumulate the matches. The shape of the program mirrors the shape of the data.
